Pay your Employers PAYE

You can pay by Direct Debit, online or telephone banking, BACS or CHAPS.

Companies with 250 or more employees must pay electronically.

It’s your responsibility to make sure payments reach HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) on time – you may have to pay a penalty and be charged interest if they don’t.

Paying PAYE and class 1 National Insurance

You need your accounts office reference number to make a payments. You can find this on:

  • the letter HMRC sent you when you first registered as an employer
  • the letter HMRC sends you instead of a payment booklet
  • the front of your payment booklet if you still get one

You have to enter the number without gaps between the characters.

All end of year balancing payments must show in month 12. You can only use month 13 to pay National Insurance class 1 you owe on your P11D.

Direct Debit

Use your company’s @{ePayeOnlineAccountLink.set(ID, "ePayeOnlineAccountHref1").set(TEXT, "HMRC online account").render} to set up a Direct Debit at least 5 working days before the payment is due.

Once the Direct Debit is set up, payments will leave your account 3 days before they’re due. They’ll show on your statements as ‘HMRC NDDS’.

You can change your Direct Debit through your @{ePayeOnlineAccountLink.set(ID, "ePayeOnlineAccountHref2").set(TEXT, "HMRC online account").render}.

Online with a debit or credit card

You can pay online with a card from a UK issuer - not American Express or Diners Club.

Allow 3 working days for payments to reach HMRC.

There’s a 1.4% fee if you pay by credit card.

Online or telephone banking, BACS, CHAPS or standing order

Check which HMRC bank account to use for payments.

Very large employers who want to pay by CHAPS should contact HMRC about multiple and composite payments.
