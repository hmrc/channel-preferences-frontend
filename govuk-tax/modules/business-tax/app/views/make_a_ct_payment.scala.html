Pay your Corporation Tax

Pay Corporation Tax by Direct Debit, online with a debit or credit card or through your bank.

It’s your responsibility to make sure payments reach HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) on time - you may be charged interest if you don't

Your Corporation Tax payment reference number

You must use the 17-character Corporation Tax payment reference number specific to the period you're paying. Your payment will be delayed if you don't.

You can find the payment reference number

  • on your payslip
  • through your company's HMRC online account

Direct Debit

Set up a Direct Debit through your company's HMRC online account.

Allow 5 working days to process a Direct Debit the first time you set one up. After that, it's 3 working days.

Payments will appear on your bank statements as "HMRC NDDS". You can make changes to the Direct Debit through your company's HMRC online account.

Online with a debit or credit card

You can pay online. You must have your Corporation Tax payment reference number.

Allow 3 working days for payments to reach HMRC.

You must use a card issued by a UK card issuer - not American Express or Diners Club.

Online or telephone banking, Bacs, CHAPS or standing order

Check your bank’s transaction limits and processing times.

The back of your payslip tells you which HMRC account to use. If you're not sure, use Cumbernauld. You must use your Corporation Tax payment reference number

If you want to pay by CHAPS, you can fill in an instruction to your bank online. CHAPS payments usually reach HMRC the same day if your bank sends them between 9am and 3pm.

Multiple CHAPS payments

To make a single payment for multiple Corporation Tax references, contact either the Cumbernauld or Shipley accounts office.

Nothing to pay

You must tell HMRC if you have nothing to pay. You will need your Corporation Tax payment reference number.

Payments for more than 1 company

If companies in a group pay under a Group Payment Arrangement HMRC will write telling you the correct payment reference.
