Your tax account

Pay your VAT bill by Direct Debit, online with a debit or credit card or through your bank.

It’s your responsibility to make sure payments reach HMRC on time. Use the payment deadline calculator to make sure you pay on time - you may be charged a penalty if your payment is late.

Direct Debit

Use your VAT online account to set up a Direct Debit at least 2 working days before your VAT Return and payment are due (or 3 working days, if these are due on a weekend or bank holiday).

HMRC will tell you when the Direct Debit has been set up. The payment deadline calculator tells you when the payment leaves your account. If you need to make changes to your Direct Debit, do this through your bank.

You must pay by another method until the Direct Debit is set up.

Annual Accounting Scheme

If you use the Annual Accounting Scheme, you can only set up automatic Direct Debit online for balancing payments. You can set up a Direct Debit for regular payments using form VAT623.

Download ‘Form VAT623, Direct Debit Instruction’ (PDF, 336KB)

Online with a debit or credit card

You can pay online - you’ll need your VAT registration number @user.userAuthority.vrn.

You must use a card from a UK card issuer - not American Express or Diners Club. Allow 3 working days for payments to reach HMRC.

There’s a 1.4% fee if you pay by credit card.

Online or telephone banking, Bacs, CHAPS or standing order

Check your bank’s transaction limits and processing times.

You need HMRC’s bank details or you can use the CHAPS form or standing order form. Use your VAT registration number as the payment reference @user.userAuthority.vrn.
