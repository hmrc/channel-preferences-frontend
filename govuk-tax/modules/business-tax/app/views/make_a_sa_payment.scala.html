Pay your Self Assessment tax bill

Options to pay your tax bill are shown below.

It’s your responsibility to make sure payments reach HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) by the @Link.toExternalPage(id = Some("deadlineHref"), url = "http://www.gov.uk/self-assessment-tax-return-deadlines", value = Some("Self Assessment deadline")).toHtml. You may have to pay @Link.toExternalPage(id = Some("penaltiesAndInterestHref"), url = "http://www.gov.uk/self-assessment-tax-returns/penalties", value = Some("penalties and interest")).toHtml if they're late.

You need your Self Assessment reference number, known as Unique Taxpayer Reference number (UTR) @utrK.

Direct Debit

@Link.toPortalPage(id = Some("btDirectDebitsHref1"), url = directDebitsUrl, value = Some("Set up a Direct Debit")).toHtml

Allow 5 working days to process a Direct Debit the first time you set one up. After that, it’s 3 working days.

Payments will appear on your bank statements as “HMRC NDDS”.

You can also @Link.toPortalPage(id = Some("btDirectDebitsHref2"), url = directDebitsUrl, value = Some("change or arrange a new Direct Debit")).toHtml online.

Flexible payments

You can make flexible payments before your tax deadline if you’re on a ‘budget payment plan’. It lets you:

  • decide how much to pay each week or month
  • stop paying for up to 6 months

@Link.toPortalPage(id = Some("btDirectDebitsHref3"), url = directDebitsUrl, value = Some("Set up a budget payment plan")).toHtml - you must be up to date with your payments and pay by Direct Debit.

If the total you’ve paid during the year doesn’t cover your bill in full, you’ll have to pay the difference.

Online with a debit or credit card

You can @Link.toExternalPage(id = Some("payOnlineHref"), url = "https://www.santanderbillpayment.co.uk/hmrc/scripts/nrpaynow.asp", value = Some("pay online by card")).toHtml. You need your UTR @utrK.

You must use a card from a UK card issuer - not American Express or Diners Club. Allow 3 working days for payments to reach HMRC.

There’s a 1.4% fee if you pay by credit card.

Online, telephone banking, Bacs or CHAPS

Check your bank’s transaction limits and processing times.

If you make a Faster Payment it will clear the same day if the amount is within your bank’s limits.

Your payslip tells you which HMRC account to use. If you’re not sure, use HMRC Cumbernauld.

You must use your UTR as the payment reference @utrK.
