Pay your Self Assessment tax bill

Pay your Self Assessment tax bill by Direct Debit, debit or credit card, budget payment plan or through your Pay As You Earn (PAYE) tax code.

It’s your responsibility to make sure payments reach HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) by the @helpers.link("deadlineHref", "http://www.gov.uk/self-assessment-tax-return-deadlines", "Self Assessment deadlines", true). You may have to pay @helpers.link("penaltiesAndInterestHref", "http://www.gov.uk/self-assessment-tax-returns/penalties", "penalties and interest", true) if it’s late.

You need your Self Assessment reference number, known as Unique Taxpayer Reference number (UTR) @utr.

Direct Debit

Use your company's HMRC online account to @helpers.link("btDirectDebitsHref1", ddLink.url, "set up a Direct Debit", ddLink.newWindow, ddLink.sso).

Allow 5 working days to process a Direct Debit the first time you set one up. After that, it’s 3 working days.

Payments will appear on your bank statements as “HMRC NDDS”.

To @helpers.link("btDirectDebitsHref2", ddLink.url, "make changes to a Direct Debit or arrange new payment", ddLink.newWindow, ddLink.sso), use your HMRC online account.

Flexible payments

You can make flexible payments if you’re on a ‘budget payment plan’. It lets you:

  • decide how much to pay each week or month
  • stop paying for up to 6 months

@helpers.link("btDirectDebitsHref3", ddLink.url, "Set up a budget payment plan", ddLink.newWindow, ddLink.sso) using your HMRC online account - you must be up to date with your payments and pay by Direct Debit.

If the total you’ve paid during the year doesn’t cover your bill in full, you’ll have to pay the difference.

Online with a debit or credit card

You can @helpers.link("payOnlineHref", "https://www.santanderbillpayment.co.uk/hmrc/scripts/nrpaynow.asp", "pay online with a card", true). You need your UTR @utr

You must use a card from a UK card issuer - not American Express or Diners Club. Allow 3 working days for payments to reach HMRC.

There’s a 1.4% fee if you pay by credit card.

Online or telephone banking, BACS, CHAPS or standing order

Check your bank’s transaction limits and processing times.

Your payslip tells you which HMRC account to use. If you’re not sure, use HMRC Cumbernauld.

You must use your UTR as the payment reference. @utr.

Your PAYE tax code

You may be able to @helpers.link("payeTaxCodeHref", "http://www.hmrc.gov.uk/payinghmrc/taxcode.htm", "pay your Self Assessment tax bill through your PAYE tax code", true) if you owe less than £3,000. The tax you owe is deducted from your salary or pension.
